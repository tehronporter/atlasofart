# Atlas of Art — Post-MVP Next Steps Doc

## Purpose
This document captures what comes after the focused MVP so those ideas do not creep into the current build.

## Next logical phase
After the MVP works, the best next phase is:
- replace or supplement local seed data with structured museum data
- improve provenance storytelling
- deepen discovery and filtering

## Strong next-step features

### 1. Getty data ingestion
- connect to real collection data
- normalize records into the Atlas artwork shape
- add a simple import pipeline

### 2. Better related works
- improve metadata scoring
- support more nuanced grouping
- add stronger cultural and time relationships

### 3. Provenance / journey layer
- connect the current museum to place of creation
- visualize movement over time

### 4. Search
- let users jump to place, culture, or artwork

### 5. Better filters
- region
- culture
- medium
- century

### 6. Saved collections
- let users save favorite works or routes of discovery

### 7. Historical overlays
- routes, empires, trade networks, or cultural zones

## Things to avoid too early
Do not immediately add:
- too many filters
- heavy AI features inside the product
- overbuilt backend architecture
- complex auth flows
- multi-role systems

## Product principle
The MVP proves one thing:
users enjoy discovering artworks through geography and time.

Everything after MVP should strengthen that core idea, not distract from it.
